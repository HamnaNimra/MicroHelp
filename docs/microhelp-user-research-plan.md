# MicroHelp - User Research Plan

**Date:** February 2, 2026  
**Author:** Hamna Nimra  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Research Timeline:** 4 weeks (pre-launch validation)

---

## Research Objectives

### Primary Objective
Validate that people in our target demographic actually want hyperlocal neighbor help and would use an app for it.

### Secondary Objectives
1. Understand current pain points with asking neighbors for help
2. Identify most common types of help requests
3. Determine acceptable response time expectations
4. Validate safety concerns and requirements
5. Test willingness to help strangers in their neighborhood
6. Identify optimal onboarding flow
7. Validate pricing/monetization tolerance (future)

---

## Research Questions

### Problem Validation
- How often do you need small favors or help? (frequency)
- What stops you from asking neighbors currently? (barriers)
- Have you ever needed help and couldn't get it? (pain severity)
- What problems has lack of neighbor connection caused? (consequences)

### Solution Validation
- Would you use an app to ask neighbors for help? (intent)
- What types of requests would you post? (use cases)
- What types of requests would you accept? (supply validation)
- How quickly would you expect a response? (expectations)
- What safety features would you need to feel comfortable? (requirements)

### Behavioral
- How do you currently ask for help? (current solutions)
- Who do you ask when you need something? (alternatives)
- Have you used Nextdoor/TaskRabbit/Buy Nothing? (competitive experience)
- What would make you trust a stranger to help you? (trust factors)

## Research Methods

### Method 1: User Interviews (Qualitative)

**Target:** 20-30 interviews  
**Duration:** 30-45 minutes each  
**Format:** Video call (Zoom) or in-person  
**Incentive:** $30 Amazon gift card

**Participant Criteria:**
- Lives in target neighborhood (pilot area)
- Age 25-45
- Lives alone OR with partner/young kids
- Moved within last 3 years OR has few nearby friends
- Tech-savvy (uses apps regularly)
- Mix of renters and homeowners
- 50% women (safety concerns are critical)

**Recruitment:**
- Post in local Facebook groups
- Flyers at coffee shops
- Nextdoor post (ironic, but effective)
- Referrals from friends
- UserInterviews.com

---

### Interview Script

**Introduction (5 min)**
"Hi [name], thanks for taking the time. I'm building a neighborhood app and want to understand how people get help with small tasks. There are no right or wrong answers - I just want to hear your honest experience. This isn't a sales pitch, I genuinely want to learn. Sound good?"

**Section 1: Current Situation (10 min)**

1. "Tell me about your neighborhood. Do you know your neighbors?"
   - Probe: How many can you name?
   - Probe: How did you meet them?

2. "When was the last time you needed a small favor or help with something?"
   - Probe: What was it?
   - Probe: Who did you ask?
   - Probe: How did it go?

3. "Have you ever needed help but couldn't get it? Tell me about that."
   - Probe: What happened?
   - Probe: How did you solve it?
   - Probe: How did that make you feel?

4. "What stops you from asking your neighbors for help?"
   - Listen for: fear of rejection, awkwardness, don't know them, reciprocity pressure

5. "Have you ever helped a neighbor? What was that like?"
   - Probe: How did they ask?
   - Probe: How did you feel about it?

**Section 2: App Concept (15 min)**

[Show mockup/prototype]

"I'm working on an app where you can post small requests like 'need to borrow jumper cables' or 'can someone water my plants?' and neighbors nearby can see it and accept. Let me show you..."

6. "What's your initial reaction to this?"
   - Let them talk, don't defend

7. "Would you use something like this? Why or why not?"
   - If yes: "What would you use it for?"
   - If no: "What would need to change?"

8. "What types of help would you ask for?"
   - Probe: Give me 3 examples
   - Probe: What's too big to ask for?

9. "What types of help would you be willing to give?"
   - Probe: What would you NOT help with?
   - Probe: How much time would you give?

10. "What concerns do you have about using this?"
    - Listen for: safety, privacy, reciprocity, flaky people

11. "What information would you need to see about someone before helping them?"
    - Probe: Age? Gender? Photo? Trust score?
    - Probe: What makes someone trustworthy?

12. "If you posted a request, how long would you wait before giving up?"
    - Probe: 5 minutes? 30 minutes? An hour?

**Section 3: Safety & Trust (10 min)**

13. [For women] "As a woman, what safety concerns would you have?"
    - Probe: What features would make you feel safer?

14. "Would you feel comfortable meeting a stranger from this app?"
    - Probe: What circumstances? Time of day? Location?

15. "How would you want the app to handle if someone doesn't show up?"
    - Listen for: reporting, trust score impact, release back to feed

**Section 4: Competitive Experience (5 min)**

16. "Have you used Nextdoor? What's your experience?"
    - Probe: Why do/don't you use it?

17. "Have you used TaskRabbit or similar? When?"
    - Probe: What worked? What didn't?

**Wrap-up (5 min)**

18. "If this app existed tomorrow, what's the first thing you'd post?"

19. "What would make you recommend this to a friend?"

20. "Anything else I should know that I didn't ask about?"

**Thank them, send gift card within 24 hours**

### Method 2: Survey (Quantitative)

**Target:** 200-300 responses  
**Duration:** 5-7 minutes  
**Format:** Google Forms / Typeform  
**Incentive:** Entry into raffle for $100 gift card

**Distribution:**
- Same channels as interview recruitment
- Easier to get volume than interviews
- Use to validate interview findings at scale

**Survey Questions:**

**Screener:**
1. What neighborhood do you live in? [Open text]
2. How long have you lived there? [<1 year, 1-3 years, 3-5 years, 5+ years]
3. Age range? [18-25, 26-35, 36-45, 46-55, 56-65, 65+]
4. Living situation? [Alone, With partner, With roommates, With family/kids]

**Problem Validation:**
5. How many of your neighbors do you know by name? [0, 1-2, 3-5, 6-10, 10+]

6. In the past 3 months, have you needed help with any of these? [Select all]
   - Borrow something (tools, ingredients, etc.)
   - Jump start car / flat tire
   - Pet care (walk dog, feed cat)
   - Plant watering
   - Package receipt
   - Moving something heavy
   - Quick advice/recommendation
   - Other: [text]
   - None of the above

7. When you needed help, who did you ask? [Select all]
   - Neighbor
   - Friend (not nearby)
   - Family
   - Paid service (TaskRabbit, etc.)
   - Did it myself
   - Went without
   - Posted online (Facebook, Nextdoor)

8. What stops you from asking neighbors for help? [Select top 3]
   - Don't know them
   - Afraid they'll say no
   - Feel like I'm bothering them
   - Worry about owing them back
   - Don't want to seem needy
   - Safety concerns
   - Nothing stops me

**Solution Validation:**
9. Would you use an app to ask neighbors for small favors? [Definitely yes, Probably yes, Maybe, Probably not, Definitely not]

10. Would you use an app to help neighbors with small favors? [Definitely yes, Probably yes, Maybe, Probably not, Definitely not]

11. What types of requests would you post? [Select all that apply]
   - Borrow items (tools, ingredients)
   - Car help (jump, flat tire)
   - Pet care
   - Plant watering
   - Package receipt
   - Moving/lifting help
   - Quick errands
   - Advice/recommendations
   - None - I wouldn't post

12. What types of requests would you accept? [Select all that apply]
   - Same list as above

13. How quickly would you expect a response? [5 min, 15 min, 30 min, 1 hour, A few hours, Doesn't matter]

14. What information would you need about someone before helping them? [Select all]
   - Photo
   - Full name
   - Age
   - Gender
   - How long they've lived there
   - How many times they've helped others
   - Verification badge (phone/email verified)
   - Reviews/ratings

**Safety:**
15. [If female] What safety features would you need? [Select all]
   - See gender before accepting
   - See age before accepting
   - Share my location with emergency contact
   - Verification required
   - Report/block features
   - Meet only during daylight
   - Other: [text]

**Competitive:**
16. Have you used any of these? [Select all]
   - Nextdoor
   - TaskRabbit
   - Buy Nothing Project
   - Thumbtack
   - Local Facebook groups
   - None of these

17. If you use Nextdoor, why? [Open text, optional]

18. If you DON'T use Nextdoor, why not? [Open text, optional]

**Willingness to Pay (Future):**
19. Would you pay for premium features? [Yes, Maybe, No]

20. If yes, what's reasonable per month? [$0, $2-5, $5-10, $10-15, $15+]

**Open Feedback:**
21. What concerns do you have about this concept? [Open text]

22. What would make you download and try this? [Open text]

### Method 3: Prototype Testing (Usability)

**Target:** 10-15 users  
**Duration:** 45-60 minutes  
**Format:** In-person or remote (Zoom + screen share)  
**Incentive:** $40 gift card

**Test Flow:**

**Setup:**
- Use Figma prototype or functional MVP
- Give user specific scenarios to complete
- Think-aloud protocol (narrate what they're doing)
- Observe without helping (painful but necessary)

**Scenarios:**

**Scenario 1: Post a Request**
"Your car won't start and you need jumper cables. Use the app to get help."

**Observe:**
- Can they find the "+" button?
- Do they understand request vs offer?
- Is 200 char limit clear?
- Do they understand radius settings?
- Do they notice anonymity toggle?

**Questions after:**
- Was anything confusing?
- What would you change?
- Did you feel confident someone would see this?

**Scenario 2: Accept a Request**
"You see a neighbor needs help moving a couch upstairs. You have 10 minutes to spare. Help them."

**Observe:**
- Can they find the post detail?
- Do they notice poster's gender/age/trust score?
- Do they understand what "accept" means?
- Is safety information clear?

**Questions after:**
- Did you feel safe accepting this?
- What information was missing?
- Would you actually do this in real life?

**Scenario 3: Chat & Complete**
"You accepted the request. Coordinate with them and mark it complete when done."

**Observe:**
- Can they find the chat?
- Is it clear how to send a message?
- Can they find "mark complete"?
- Do they understand both people must confirm?

**Questions after:**
- Was chatting intuitive?
- How would you share your exact address?
- What happens if they don't show up?

**General Usability:**
- System Usability Scale (SUS) questionnaire
- Net Promoter Score: "How likely are you to recommend this to a friend?" (0-10)

### Method 4: Behavioral Observation (Ethnographic)

**Target:** 5-10 participants  
**Duration:** 1 week per participant  
**Format:** Diary study + follow-up interview  
**Incentive:** $50 gift card

**Process:**
1. Recruit participants who live in pilot neighborhood
2. Ask them to document every time they need help over 1 week
3. Provide diary template (Google Form or Notion)
4. Follow-up interview to discuss findings

**Diary Template:**

After each incident, log:
- Date/time
- What did you need help with?
- Who did you ask? (neighbor, friend, service, no one)
- Why did you choose that person/method?
- How long did it take to get help?
- How did you feel about the experience?
- Would you have used an app like MicroHelp? Why/why not?

**Follow-up Interview Questions:**
- Looking back at your week, what surprised you?
- Were there patterns you noticed?
- When would an app have been useful?
- When would you NOT have used an app?

**Goal:** Understand real behavior, not hypothetical. What people say vs what they do often differ.

## Analysis Framework

### Qualitative Analysis (Interviews)

**Process:**
1. Record all interviews (with permission)
2. Transcribe using Otter.ai or Rev
3. Code transcripts using thematic analysis
4. Use Dovetail or Notion for organizing insights

**Themes to Look For:**
- Barriers to asking (fear, awkwardness, don't know neighbors)
- Safety concerns (especially from women)
- Trust factors (what makes someone trustworthy)
- Use cases (most common requests)
- Competitive gaps (what Nextdoor/TaskRabbit don't solve)
- Feature requests
- Deal-breakers (would prevent usage)

**Deliverable:**
- Research report with themes + participant quotes
- Affinity map of insights
- Persona updates based on findings

---

### Quantitative Analysis (Survey)

**Metrics to Calculate:**
- **Problem severity:** % who needed help in last 3 months
- **Current solution gaps:** % who went without help or used expensive alternatives
- **Intent to use:** % who said "definitely yes" or "probably yes"
- **Supply/demand match:** Do people want to help with what others need?
- **Safety needs:** What % need gender visibility, verification, etc.
- **Competitive usage:** How many use Nextdoor? Why/why not?

**Segmentation:**
- By age group (are younger users more interested?)
- By living situation (alone vs with family)
- By gender (do women have different needs?)
- By tenure (new residents vs long-term)

**Statistical Tests:**
- Chi-square for categorical differences (gender, age)
- Correlation between variables (knowing neighbors â†’ willingness to help)

**Deliverable:**
- Survey results deck with key findings
- Visualizations (charts, graphs)
- Statistical significance where relevant

---

### Prototype Testing Analysis

**Metrics:**
- **Task completion rate:** Did they finish the scenario?
- **Time to complete:** How long did each task take?
- **Error rate:** How many mistakes/wrong taps?
- **System Usability Scale score:** Industry standard (68 = average)
- **Net Promoter Score:** Likelihood to recommend

**Qualitative:**
- Points of confusion (where did they get stuck?)
- Missing information (what did they look for that wasn't there?)
- Feature requests
- Emotional reactions (frustration, delight, confusion)

**Deliverable:**
- Usability report with specific UX improvements
- Prioritized list of fixes (critical, high, medium, low)

## Success Criteria

### Go/No-Go Decision

**GREEN LIGHT (Build It):**
- 60%+ say "definitely/probably yes" to using it
- 70%+ have needed help in last 3 months
- 50%+ would both post AND accept requests (supply/demand balance)
- Clear use cases identified (top 5 request types)
- Safety concerns are addressable with planned features
- SUS score >68 (usable)
- NPS >40 (would recommend)

**YELLOW LIGHT (Pivot/Iterate):**
- 40-60% would use it
- Limited use cases OR limited willingness to help
- Major safety concerns we haven't addressed
- SUS score 50-68 (needs UX work)
- NPS 20-40 (neutral)

**RED LIGHT (Don't Build):**
- <40% would use it
- No supply (people won't help)
- Insurmountable safety concerns
- SUS score <50 (unusable)
- NPS <20 (would not recommend)
- People prefer current solutions

## Research Risks & Mitigation

### Risk 1: Can't recruit enough participants
**Mitigation:**
- Increase incentive ($40 instead of $30)
- Recruit outside target neighborhood if needed
- Use UserInterviews.com (paid recruitment)
- Post in multiple channels

### Risk 2: Positive response bias
**Mitigation:**
- Emphasize "no wrong answers"
- Ask about actual behavior, not hypotheticals
- Include diary study (real behavior)
- Look for contradictions between what people say vs do

### Risk 3: Results show no one wants this
**Mitigation:**
- That's actually valuable! Don't build it.
- Identify pivot opportunities
- Save months of wasted development

### Risk 4: Results are inconclusive
**Mitigation:**
- Do more research (extend timeline)
- Test prototype in real neighborhood (beta)
- Start small pilot and measure actual behavior

## Post-Research Actions

### If GREEN LIGHT:
1. Update PRD based on findings
2. Prioritize features based on user needs
3. Adjust safety features based on concerns raised
4. Refine personas
5. Proceed to development

### If YELLOW LIGHT:
1. Identify specific issues
2. Design solutions
3. Test solutions with users
4. Iterate until green light

### If RED LIGHT:
1. Document why it failed
2. Identify pivot opportunities
3. Decide: pivot or kill project
4. Don't waste time building something no one wants

## Ongoing Research (Post-Launch)

Research doesn't stop at launch. Continue learning:

**Week 1-4 Post-Launch:**
- Weekly user interviews (5 per week)
- In-app survey after first help completed
- Monitor support tickets (common issues?)
- Track behavioral metrics (match survey predictions?)

**Monthly:**
- NPS survey
- Feature request survey
- Churn interviews (why did they leave?)

**Quarterly:**
- Deep-dive research on specific features
- Competitive landscape check
- User persona updates
