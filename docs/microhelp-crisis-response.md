## Purpose

This document outlines how to respond when things go wrong. Crises will happen. Having a plan means responding quickly, transparently, and effectively to minimize damage.

**Types of Crises Covered:**
1. Safety Incident (assault, theft, harm)
2. PR Crisis (negative press, viral complaints)
3. Data Breach (user data compromised)
4. App Outage (Firebase down, can't access app)
5. Legal Action (lawsuit, regulatory issue)

## General Crisis Response Framework

### Phase 1: Detection & Assessment (First 30 Minutes)

**Step 1: Confirm the crisis is real**
- Multiple sources reporting?
- Verified vs rumor?
- Severity level?

**Step 2: Assemble crisis team**
- Emergency Slack channel activated
- All hands on deck
- Cancel non-essential meetings

**Step 3: Assess severity**

**Severity Levels:**

**Level 1 (Minor):** Single user issue, no press, low impact
- Example: One user reports another user was rude
- Response: Support team handles, no escalation needed

**Level 2 (Moderate):** Multiple users affected, potential press, medium impact
- Example: App crashes for 2 hours during peak time
- Response: Fix immediately, post status update, apologize

**Level 3 (Major):** Many users affected, press coverage likely, high impact
- Example: User assaulted during a help exchange
- Response: Full crisis mode, legal involved, public statement

**Level 4 (Critical):** Existential threat to company, major press, potential shutdown
- Example: Major data breach, criminal investigation
- Response: All-hands crisis response, CEO statement, possible pause of service

---

### Phase 2: Containment (First 2 Hours)

**Step 1: Stop the bleeding**
- Disable affected features if needed
- Remove harmful content immediately
- Suspend problematic users
- Take app offline if necessary (extreme cases only)

**Step 2: Gather facts**
- Who is affected?
- How many people?
- What exactly happened?
- Timeline of events
- Screenshots/evidence

**Step 3: Internal communication**
- Brief entire team (even if small)
- Aligned on facts
- No one talks to press without approval

---

### Phase 3: Response (First 24 Hours)

**Step 1: User communication**
- In-app banner (if app is up)
- Email to affected users
- Social media post (if appropriate)
- Status page update

**Step 2: Public statement (if Level 3+)**
- Acknowledge what happened
- Express empathy/concern
- Explain what we're doing
- Commit to updates
- NEVER speculate or blame victim

**Step 3: Press response (if contacted)**
- Prepared statement only
- No improvising
- Refer to statement
- "We're cooperating with authorities" (if applicable)

---

### Phase 4: Resolution (First Week)

**Step 1: Fix the root cause**
- Implement technical fixes
- Update policies
- Add safety features
- Ban problematic users permanently

**Step 2: Follow-up communication**
- Update on resolution
- New safety measures implemented
- Transparency about what we learned

**Step 3: Support affected users**
- Direct outreach
- Offer support resources
- Refunds/credits if applicable (future, when we charge)

---

### Phase 5: Post-Mortem (After Crisis Ends)

**Step 1: Analyze what happened**
- Root cause analysis
- Timeline reconstruction
- What went wrong
- What went right

**Step 2: Prevent recurrence**
- Update policies
- Implement new features
- Train team
- Update this crisis plan

**Step 3: Document lessons learned**
- Share with team
- Update processes
- Build stronger systems

## Crisis Type #1: Safety Incident

### Scenario: User Harmed During Help Exchange

**Examples:**
- Physical assault
- Sexual harassment/assault
- Theft
- Threats/intimidation
- Stalking after help exchange

**Severity:** Level 3-4 (Major to Critical)

---

### Immediate Response (First 30 Minutes)

**Step 1: Ensure victim safety**
- If they're still in danger, advise to call 911 immediately
- "Your safety is the priority. If you're in danger, call 911 now. We're here to support you after."

**Step 2: Gather information**
- What happened? (timeline, location, nature of incident)
- Who was involved? (user IDs, real names)
- Is victim safe now?
- Have they contacted police?
- Do they need resources? (support hotlines, medical care)

**Step 3: Document everything**
- Screenshot all messages between users
- Save post details
- Export chat logs
- Preserve evidence (don't delete anything)

**Step 4: Immediate platform actions**
- Suspend accused user's account (pending investigation)
- Remove their posts from feed
- Prevent them from accessing app
- Note: "Suspended" not "Banned" until investigation complete

---

### Communication Templates

**To Victim (Within 1 Hour):**

```
Subject: MicroHelp Safety Team - Your Safety is Our Priority

[Name],

We received your report and are deeply concerned about what happened. Your safety and wellbeing are our absolute priority.

Immediate actions we've taken:
- Suspended the other user's account
- Preserved all evidence for authorities
- Flagged their account for permanent ban pending investigation

What we're doing next:
- Full investigation of the incident
- Cooperating with law enforcement if you've filed a report
- Reviewing our safety features to prevent this from happening again

Resources available to you:
[Include relevant hotlines: RAINN, local crisis centers, etc.]

Please don't hesitate to reach out if you need anything. We're here to support you.

[Personal phone number for direct contact]

Hamna
Founder, MicroHelp
```

**To Accused User (Within 2 Hours):**

```
Subject: Account Suspension - Serious Violation Report

[Name],

Your MicroHelp account has been suspended due to a serious safety violation report.

We take all reports extremely seriously. Your account is suspended pending investigation.

If you believe this is in error, you may respond to this email with your perspective within 48 hours.

MicroHelp Safety Team
```

---

### Public Statement (If Press Inquires):

**Template for Level 3 Incident:**

```
Statement from MicroHelp:

We are aware of a serious incident involving two users of our platform. The safety and wellbeing of our community is our highest priority.

Actions we've taken:
- Immediately suspended the account in question
- Are cooperating fully with law enforcement
- Have reached out to support the affected individual
- Are conducting a full review of the incident

We are committed to learning from this and strengthening our safety features. We will share more details as we're able.

Our hearts go out to everyone affected.

Hamna Nimra
Founder, MicroHelp
[email]
```

**What NOT to say:**
- "This is an isolated incident" (you don't know that yet)
- "We've never had problems before" (sounds defensive)
- Anything that blames the victim
- Details about the incident (privacy + legal risk)
- Specifics about the users involved

---

### Legal Coordination

**Step 1: Contact lawyer immediately**
- Brief them on facts
- Get advice on statement
- Understand liability

**Step 2: Cooperate with law enforcement**
- Provide data if subpoenaed
- Don't volunteer more than required
- Document all interactions

**Step 3: Review Terms of Service liability**
- Are we protected?
- What's our exposure?
- Insurance claim if applicable

---

### Post-Incident Actions

**Within 1 Week:**
1. Conduct full investigation
2. Permanent ban if incident confirmed
3. Update safety features based on learnings
4. Offer victim support resources
5. Communicate changes to community

**New Safety Features to Consider:**
- Emergency contact integration
- Location sharing with trusted contact
- Video verification (Phase 3)
- Background check tier (Phase 3)
- Safety check-in reminders
- Block/report improvements

## Crisis Type #2: PR Crisis (Viral Negative Coverage)

### Scenario: Negative Tweet/Article Goes Viral

**Examples:**
- "MicroHelp enables [bad thing]" thread goes viral
- Local news investigates "dangerous new app"
- Influencer with large following posts bad experience
- Reddit thread dunking on the app

**Severity:** Level 2-3 (Moderate to Major)

---

### Immediate Response (First Hour)

**Step 1: Assess the damage**
- How many people saw it?
- Is it spreading?
- What's the claim? (true, false, or partially true?)
- Who's the source? (verified account, journalist, influencer?)

**Step 2: Verify facts**
- Is the complaint legitimate?
- Did we mess up?
- Is there a technical bug?
- Is this a misunderstanding?

**Step 3: Decide response strategy**

**If complaint is TRUE:**
- Acknowledge mistake
- Apologize sincerely
- Explain what we're fixing
- Never defensive

**If complaint is FALSE:**
- Politely correct with facts
- Show evidence
- Stay calm, not combative
- Don't engage in argument

**If complaint is PARTIALLY TRUE:**
- Acknowledge the valid parts
- Correct the false parts
- Commit to doing better

---

### Response Templates

**For Legitimate Complaint:**

```
You're right, and we messed up. [Specifically acknowledge what went wrong]

Here's what we're doing to fix it:
[Concrete actions, timeline]

We should have done better. Thanks for holding us accountable.
```

**For False Claim:**

```
We hear your concern, but want to clarify the facts:
[Correct with evidence, screenshots, data]

We're always open to feedback, but wanted to make sure the full picture is clear.
```

---

### When to Respond Publicly vs Privately

**Respond Publicly (Twitter/Reddit/etc):**
- Major influencer (100K+ followers)
- False information spreading widely
- Legitimate issue affecting many users
- Can demonstrate we're responsive

**Respond Privately (DM/Email):**
- Small account, limited reach
- Sensitive personal issue
- Better chance of resolution in private
- Don't want to amplify complaint

**Don't Respond At All:**
- Obvious troll
- No constructive criticism
- Engaging will make it worse
- Already addressed publicly

---

### Press Inquiry Response

**If Journalist Contacts You:**

**Step 1: Buy time**
"Thanks for reaching out. I want to make sure I give you accurate information. Can I get back to you by [specific time today]?"

**Step 2: Prepare statement**
- Review with lawyer if legal issues
- Get facts straight
- Draft clear, concise response

**Step 3: Provide statement**
- Stick to facts
- Don't speculate
- Be transparent where possible
- Offer to follow up

**Template:**

```
Thanks for your inquiry about [issue].

[Acknowledge the concern]
[State the facts]
[Explain what we're doing]
[Commit to transparency]

We're a small team building something we believe in, and we take feedback seriously. We're committed to building a safe, useful product for our community.

Happy to answer any other questions.

Hamna Nimra
Founder, MicroHelp
```

## Crisis Type #3: Data Breach

### Scenario: User Data Compromised

**Examples:**
- Firebase security rules misconfigured, data exposed
- Hacker gains access to database
- Employee accidentally exposes data
- Third-party service (Twilio, etc.) breached

**Severity:** Level 4 (Critical)

---

### Immediate Response (First 30 Minutes)

**Step 1: Stop the breach**
- Lock down Firebase immediately
- Revoke all API keys
- Force logout all users
- Disable compromised services

**Step 2: Assess scope**
- What data was accessed?
- How many users affected?
- What's the worst-case scenario?
- Is data still being accessed? (close the hole)

**Step 3: Notify legal counsel**
- Legal requirements vary by state/country
- GDPR, CCPA, other regulations
- Timeline for required notifications
- Potential fines/penalties

---

### Legal Requirements

**US (varies by state):**
- Must notify users "without unreasonable delay"
- Some states require notification within 30-90 days
- Must notify authorities in some cases

**GDPR (if EU users):**
- Must notify authorities within 72 hours
- Must notify users "without undue delay"
- Significant penalties for non-compliance

**CCPA (California):**
- Must notify within specific timeframe
- Class action risk if negligent

**Action: Consult lawyer immediately to understand requirements**

---

---

### Post-Breach Actions

**Within 1 Week:**
1. Full security audit (hire external firm)
2. Implement all recommended fixes
3. Force password resets
4. Add 2FA option
5. Monitor for fraud/misuse
6. Set up credit monitoring for affected users (if PII exposed)

**Within 1 Month:**
1. Publish detailed post-mortem (what happened, why, how we fixed it)
2. Implement ongoing security practices
3. Regular penetration testing
4. Security training for team
5. Incident response drills

**Examples:**
- Firebase regional outage
- Database hits quota limits
- Catastrophic bug

- Error logs
- Recent deployments

## Crisis Type #5: Legal Action

### Scenario: Lawsuit Filed or Regulatory Investigation

**Examples:**
- User sues over injury
- State AG investigates safety practices
- Class action over data breach
- Cease & desist from competitor

**Severity:** Level 3-4 (Major to Critical)

---

### Immediate Response (Same Day)

**Step 1: Contact lawyer immediately**
- Do NOT respond to legal documents without counsel
- Do NOT post about it publicly
- Do NOT discuss with team beyond leadership

**Step 2: Preserve evidence**
- Don't delete anything
- Collect all relevant data
- Document timeline of events

**Step 3: Follow lawyer's advice**
- They'll guide response strategy
- May advise silence
- May advise proactive statement

---

### Public Communication

**Generally:**
- Say very little
- "We're aware of the legal matter and reviewing it with counsel"
- "We don't comment on pending litigation"
- Never admit liability publicly

**Only if Lawyer Approves:**
- Brief factual statement
- No speculation
- No details
- Refer to statement

## Crisis Preparation (Before Crisis Hits)

### Insurance
- General liability insurance
- Cyber insurance (data breach coverage)
- D&O insurance (if incorporated)

### Legal
- Lawyer on retainer
- Terms of Service reviewed
- Privacy Policy compliant with laws
- Liability waivers where appropriate

### Technical
- Regular security audits
- Penetration testing
- Backup systems
- Monitoring/alerting

### Communication
- Status page set up (statuspage.io)
- Crisis communication templates prepared
- Press contact list maintained
- Team trained on protocols

## Post-Crisis Recovery

### Rebuilding Trust

**Step 1: Transparency**
- Publish full post-mortem
- Explain what went wrong
- What we learned
- What we changed

**Step 2: Actions, Not Just Words**
- Implement new safety features
- Regular security updates
- Community check-ins

**Step 3: Consistency**
- Deliver on promises
- Don't let it happen again
- Prove through behavior
